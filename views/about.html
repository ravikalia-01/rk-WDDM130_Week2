History and Development of Express.js

Express.js is a fast, minimal, and flexible web framework for Node.js, designed for building web applications and APIs. It was created by TJ Holowaychuk and released in 2010. Express became the de facto standard server framework for Node.js due to its simplicity, extensibility, and large community support.

Over the years, Express has become the foundational component in many stacks such as the MEAN and MERN stacks, where it serves as the back-end layer to power web and mobile apps.

Express is maintained by the OpenJS Foundation and is open-source on GitHub. Its development continues with community contributions, focusing on performance, extensibility, and security.

For official documentation, visit the Express.js website. It offers comprehensive guides, API reference, and tutorials for both beginners and advanced developers.
